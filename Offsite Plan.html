Monday · 1:30 – 3pm

One Customer,
Many Worlds.

90 minutes. One scenario. Every function in the company in one room, each seeing it from their own seat.

A scenario brief goes in front of the room. Each function takes 8 minutes to write down privately what that situation costs their team. Then each team shares, two minutes each, with everyone else just listening. Then the whole room talks about what they heard.

Opening · 5 minSet the frame

Papers face-down on the table, unread. Say something like:

We're going to spend the next 90 minutes on one customer. Not a real customer by name, but one that'll feel familiar. What I want us to do is look at the same situation from where each of us actually sits. Engineering, CX, product, sales, Sandeep, every seat in this company. Because I don't think we always know what the person next to us is carrying. Today we find out.

Flip the papers over. Give people two minutes to read.

Phase 1 · 8 minIndividual reflection
Take 8 minutes. Don't talk to anyone yet. Write down: what does this situation cost your team specifically? Not the solution, what's the weight? What are you managing that nobody else in this room probably thinks about?
Phase 2 · 30 minRound-robin shares
5 min per team
Each team gets 5 minutes to share what they wrote. One rule: no responses, no questions, no "yes, and." Just listen. You'll get your turn.
01CX first, closest to the customer relationship
02Engineering, the technical weight beneath every customer request
03Product, the prioritisation pressure nobody outside product fully sees
04Sales, the market implications of getting this right or wrong
05Sandeep last, his view lands differently after everyone else has spoken
Phase 3 · 20 minOpen synthesis

After the last share, 10 to 15 seconds of silence. Then:

What did you hear that you didn't expect?

Open floor. Follow up if the room goes quiet.

If you need to get things moving
Is there something another team does that you depend on more than you've ever said out loud?
What would make your job genuinely easier that isn't a product or a tool?
If this customer had a great quarter, who in this room made that happen and would they know it?
Closing · 10 minOne sentence, then done
Before we finish, one sentence each. What's one thing you heard today that you're going to carry?

Go around. Quick, voluntary, low pressure. Krishna closes last. Then stand up and signal it's done.
